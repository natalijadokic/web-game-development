The difference between these results shows an important feature of data analysis. Even when researchers work with the same dataset, different decisions about data cleaning, variable selection, and statistical modeling can lead to different conclusions. This phenomenon is known as analytical variability.

Analytical variability can be understood as both a limitation and a strength of empirical research. On one hand, analytical flexibility may reduce the reproducibility of findings, as different analytical choices can lead to different conclusions even when based on the same data. On the other hand, analytical variability also reflects the complexity of data analysis and allows researchers to examine data from multiple perspectives. This flexibility can contribute to a better understanding of the phenomena when applied transparently.

For this reason, transparency in the analysis process is essential. Clearly reporting analytical decisions and, where possible, preregistering analysis plans can help reduce uncertainty and improve the interpretability of research findings.
